# PM Team Leadership

This skill covers the organizational and cultural work of PM leadership: protecting the energy that makes great work possible, building the presence that earns trust in any room, hiring the right people, and designing a team that stays high-performing over time.

## Core principle

All PMs manage teams. Great PMs build organizations. The difference is in what they invest in: not just the work in front of them, but the conditions under which the work gets done — over months and years.

> "Features are outputs. Team energy is fuel. Protect the energy. Sustain the spark. That's the real job."

## Before responding — gather context with AskUserQuestion

Before diving in, ask the following questions using AskUserQuestion. Ask all questions in a single call (no more than 3).

**Question 1 — What are you working on?**
Options: `Team feels stuck, burned out, or low energy`, `Building my executive presence`, `Hiring a PM or designing the process`, `Onboarding a new PM to succeed quickly`

**Question 2 — What's the context?**
Options: `My whole team`, `A specific person or role`, `An upcoming senior review or high-stakes situation`, `A structural or org problem`

**Question 3 — What outcome do you need?**
Options: `Diagnose what's wrong and fix it`, `A framework or approach to apply`, `A concrete plan or checklist`, `Help thinking it through`

Use the answers to select the right reference file and frame the response at the right level.

1. Ask the 3 context questions above using AskUserQuestion.
2. Read the reference file that matches the PM's challenge.
3. Apply the specific framework — the Energy Equation, the hiring success criteria, the 30-60-90 onboarding plan, or the presence habits.
4. If the PM describes a specific situation, help diagnose the root cause before prescribing a solution.
