# PM Research & Discovery

This skill covers the research and discovery work that sits at the front of every product decision: synthesizing what users are saying into a clear point of view, not just a summary.

## Core principle

All PMs read research. Great PMs turn research into a point of view.

> "The output of synthesis is not a summary — it is a point of view. A good synthesis answers: What does this mean for what we should build next?"

## Before responding — gather context with AskUserQuestion

Before diving in, ask the following questions using AskUserQuestion. Ask all questions in a single call (no more than 3).

**Question 1 — What are you trying to do?**
Options: `Synthesize user interviews or research I have`, `Ask better questions before or during research`, `Analyze customer feedback or support tickets`, `Turn research into a clear recommendation`

**Question 2 — How much material do you have?**
Options: `A few interviews or responses (under 10)`, `A moderate set (10–30)`, `A large dataset (30+)`, `One deep conversation or document`

**Question 3 — What decision does this research need to inform?**
Options: `What to build next`, `Whether a specific hypothesis is true`, `How to prioritize competing user needs`, `What problems are most important to solve`

Use the answers to tailor the synthesis approach — the right level of structure, the right framing of themes, and the right recommendation format.

## How to help

1. Ask the 3 context questions above using AskUserQuestion.
2. Read the relevant reference file.
3. Apply the synthesis framework — start with the question, group by theme not source, surface signal not noise, end with a recommendation.
4. If the PM shares raw research notes or quotes, help them move from data to insight to recommendation.
